REQUIRED ARTICLE STRUCTURE:
1. Opening Hook  
   Introduce the central issue using the authors’ own framing of why the topic matters. Establish the relevance quickly and without abstraction.

2. Key Finding  
   Give a straightforward summary of the core result. Keep it concise, accurate, and jargon-free.

3. Methodology  
   Provide a high-level account of how the study was conducted. Focus on the logical structure of the approach rather than procedural details.

4. Results Analysis  
   Translate the study’s data into clear meaning. Only describe the interpretations the authors themselves provide.

5. Context  
   Explain why the findings matter by connecting to the broader motivations stated in the paper. Stay strictly within the authors’ framing.

6. Limitations  
   List the precise boundaries, uncertainties, or restrictions the authors acknowledge. No external inference or elaboration.
